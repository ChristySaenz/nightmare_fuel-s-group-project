What is Maternal Mortality?

The U.S Centers for Disease Control and Prevention states that Maternal Mortality includes outcomes of labor and delivery that result in significant short- or long-term consequences to a woman's health.

    Severe Maternal Mortality is most likely to occur for Women who are:

  • 20 years or younger
  • 40 years or older
  • Citizens of low-income counties or districts.

Bar Chart

This tall chart is meant to signify the Overall % of population lost due to Maternal Mortality from 2000 to 2017

  • Here we can See the MMR rates are the highest In North and Sub saharan Africa
  • Followed closely by East Asia, Latin America and Oceania
  • Despite the loss of life being significant, The low deaths in more developed nations shed a ray of light.
  • Better availabilities of medical infrastructre and awareness about reproductive health has enabled more developed nations to reduce the deaths caused by Maternal Mortality overall.
